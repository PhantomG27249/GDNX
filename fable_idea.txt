This is fun — and timely, since Gated DeltaNet-2 only came out in May. Quick grounding first, then the derivation. Gated DeltaNet-2 (Hatamizadeh, Choi & Kautz, arXiv 2605.22791) generalizes Gated DeltaNet and Kimi Delta Attention by keeping adaptive forgetting and channel-wise decay while fixing their shared flaw: a single scalar gate controlling both how much old content to erase on the key side and how much new content to commit on the value side. It replaces that with a channel-wise erase gate $b_t$ and a channel-wise write gate $w_t$, trained via a chunkwise WY algorithm with the channel-wise decay absorbed into asymmetric erase factors. Notably, the GDN-2 paper already benchmarks against Mamba-3 in both SISO and MIMO variants — so MIMO exists in this design space, but nobody has yet put MIMO *inside* the decoupled erase/write delta rule, and Kronecker structure is the tool that makes that combination cheap. Here's a framework for doing exactly that. Call it **KMD-2** (Kronecker-MIMO Delta-2). This is a research proposal, not established results — treat everything below GDN-2's published update rule as a hypothesis to test.

## 1. Baseline structure

GDN-2's per-token update on the matrix state $S_t \in \mathbb{R}^{d_v \times d_k}$ has the schematic form

$$S_t = \underbrace{\big(S_{t-1}\,\mathrm{Diag}(a_t)\big)}_{\text{channel-wise decay}}\underbrace{\big(I - k_t (b_t \odot k_t)^\top\big)}_{\text{channel-wise erase}} + \underbrace{(w_t \odot v_t)\,k_t^\top}_{\text{channel-wise write}}$$

Two structural limitations remain even after decoupling erase from write. First, every token performs exactly **one rank-1 edit**, so a token carrying multiple facts must smear them into one outer product, and per-token compute is a low-arithmetic-intensity outer product rather than a matmul. Second, the joint gating of the state is **Kronecker-rank-1**: the multiplicative mask on $\mathrm{vec}(S)$ implied by $w_t$ and $b_t$ is exactly $\mathrm{Diag}(b_t)\otimes\mathrm{Diag}(w_t)$, meaning the model can't say "protect value-channels 1–8 *only for* key-directions A, but erase them for key-directions B." MIMO attacks the first problem; Kronecker factorization attacks the second (and buys you a bigger state for free).

## 2. Component A — MIMO delta rule with decoupled gates

Give each token $r$ simultaneous memory slots: project $x_t$ to $K_t \in \mathbb{R}^{d_k\times r}$, $V_t \in \mathbb{R}^{d_v\times r}$, with per-slot gate matrices $B_t \in \mathbb{R}^{d_k\times r}$ (erase) and $W_t \in \mathbb{R}^{d_v\times r}$ (write). The rank-$r$ generalization of the erase step is a **block Householder**:

$$S_t = S_{t-1}\,\mathrm{Diag}(a_t)\,\big(I - \tilde{K}_t\, T_t\, \tilde{K}_t^\top\big) + (W_t \odot V_t)\,K_t^\top$$

where $\tilde{K}_t$ carries the erase gating ($\tilde{K}_t = B_t \odot K_t$ column-wise, matching GDN-2's key-side erase) and $T_t \in \mathbb{R}^{r\times r}$ is a small triangular mixing matrix. This $T_t$ is the compact-WY "T-factor" from blocked Householder QR in numerical linear algebra, and it matters: without it, the $r$ erase directions within one token interfere with each other; with it, the block edit is equivalent to applying $r$ sequential gated delta steps, but computed as one matmul. If you make $T_t = (\epsilon I + \tilde{K}_t^\top \tilde{K}_t)^{-1}$ you recover a recursive-least-squares-style exact multi-association overwrite — a natural "DeltaNet-3" limit — at the cost of an $r\times r$ solve, which is trivial for $r \in \{4,\dots,16\}$.

Why this should help: the delta rule's whole advantage is precise editing under interference, and GDN-2's ablations show the erase gate accounts for most of its gain, consistent with selectively protecting or revising key-side associations. MIMO multiplies the number of protected/revised associations per token by $r$, and it converts outer-products into $(d\times r)(r\times d)$ matmuls, raising arithmetic intensity on tensor cores — the same motivation Mamba-3 used for its MIMO variant, now transplanted into a stronger memory-editing rule.

## 3. Component B — Kronecker factorization, in three places

**B1. Kronecker keys for state expansion.** Factor $d_k = m \cdot n$ and produce keys/queries as $k_t = k_t^{(1)} \otimes k_t^{(2)}$ with $k^{(1)}\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $k^{(2)}\in\mathbb{R}^n$. The state grows to $d_v \times mn$ (bigger fixed memory → less interference, which is exactly where linear attention loses to softmax on multi-key retrieval), while projection cost stays $O(d(m+n))$. The critical efficiency identity is the mixed-product property:

$$(k^{(1)}\otimes k^{(2)})^\top (q^{(1)}\otimes q^{(2)}) = (k^{(1)\top} q^{(1)})\,(k^{(2)\top} q^{(2)})$$

so every Gram matrix inside the chunkwise algorithm ($K^\top K$, $K^\top Q$) becomes a **Hadamard product of two small Gram matrices**. You get an $mn$-dimensional key space while never materializing $mn$-dimensional dot products in the intra-chunk attention-like matrices.

**B2. Kronecker-rank-ρ joint gating.** Generalize GDN-2's separable mask to $M_t = \sum_{i=1}^{\rho} w_t^{(i)} b_t^{(i)\top}$ applied elementwise to the state (equivalently $\sum_i \mathrm{Diag}(b^{(i)})\otimes\mathrm{Diag}(w^{(i)})$ on $\mathrm{vec}(S)$). GDN-2 is $\rho=1$. Even $\rho=2$ lets the erase pattern depend jointly on key- and value-coordinates, which is the natural next rung on the ladder scalar → channel-wise → separable → low-rank-joint. Cost is $\rho$ extra small gate projections; the mask stays a cheap elementwise op.

**B3. Kronecker-factored gate projections.** With $d_k = mn$ expanded, channel-wise gates would naively need $d\times mn$ projections. Factor them too: $a_t = a_t^{(1)}\otimes a_t^{(2)}$, $b_t = b_t^{(1)}\otimes b_t^{(2)}$. Because these are Kronecker products of *diagonals*, the two-sided decay applies as two small side-multiplications on the reshaped state, and — importantly for training — diagonal Kronecker factors commute cleanly through the WY erase factors.

## 4. Making training work: block-WY with factored decay

The reason GDN-2 is trainable at scale is its chunkwise WY form. KMD-2 preserves it. Within a chunk of length $C$, the product of block-Householder erase operators has a compact representation $\prod_t (I - \tilde{K}_t T_t \tilde{K}_t^\top) = I - U\,\Lambda\,U^\top$-style form where $U \in \mathbb{R}^{d_k \times Cr}$ stacks gated keys — this is literally blocked-WY accumulation from LAPACK-style QR, so the math is well-trodden. The channel-wise (now Kronecker-factored) decay gets absorbed the same way GDN-2 absorbs it: into asymmetric rank-one erase factors within a chunkwise parallel formulation implemented in custom Triton kernels — except the factors are now rank-$r$ blocks, and every Gram matrix in the recurrence is computed as a Hadamard product of $m$- and $n$-dimensional Grams via B1. Net effect per chunk: the sequential dependency is $C/1$ token steps collapsed into $C/r$-effective block steps of dense matmuls, with intra-chunk cost dominated by $(Cr \times d)$ GEMMs — a better shape for H100s than GDN-2's rank-1 stream.

## 5. What to measure, against what

To make claims credible, mirror GDN-2's own protocol: 1.3B parameters on 100B FineWeb-Edu tokens, matched recurrent state size, compared against Mamba-2, Gated DeltaNet, KDA, and Mamba-3 (SISO and MIMO), in both recurrent-only and hybrid settings. Two comparison regimes matter and must be kept separate: **iso-state** (shrink $d_v$ so total state matches GDN-2 — tests whether MIMO+joint gating uses capacity better) and **iso-FLOPs** (let the Kronecker state grow — tests whether cheap capacity wins). The headline hypothesis targets are multi-key NIAH in RULER and MQAR-style synthetics, since GDN-2's advantage is most pronounced in interference-heavy multi-key needle-in-a-haystack settings and every component here (more edits per token, less gate coupling, more state) attacks interference directly. Ablate one axis at a time: $r \in \{1,2,4,8\}$, $\rho \in \{1,2,4\}$, Kron keys on/off, exact-RLS $T_t$ vs. triangular $T_t$.

## 6. Honest risk register

Kronecker keys constrain the reshaped key to rank 1 in $\mathbb{R}^{m\times n}$, which may cost expressivity per key even as it buys dimensionality — mitigate with a sum of two Kronecker terms if retrieval synthetics regress. The RLS-style $T_t$ inverse can go ill-conditioned; the triangular WY construction avoids inversion entirely and should be the default. MIMO's known failure mode (from the Mamba-3 experience) is that naive slot projections learn redundant slots — a small orthogonality penalty on $K_t$'s columns, or tying slots to distinct sub-projections, is the standard fix. And the engineering cost is real: the Triton backward pass through block-WY with factored decay is the hardest 20% of this project and roughly 80% of the work.

The nice property of the whole framework is that it's a **strict generalization ladder**, in the same spirit GDN-2 itself was built: set $r=1$, $\rho=1$, $m=d_k$, $n=1$ and you recover GDN-2 exactly; collapse the gates further and you recover KDA, then Gated DeltaNet. That means you can initialize KMD-2 at the GDN-2 point and let training open up the new degrees of freedom — which also makes negative results interpretable.
